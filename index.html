AP Music Theory

  • Fundamentals of Music Notation: Staff, clefs (treble, bass, alto, tenor), note values, rests, ties, dots, time signatures (simple, compound, irregular), key signatures and the circle of fifths, accidentals
  • Scales and Modes: Major, minor (natural, harmonic, melodic), chromatic, whole tone, and church modes (Dorian, Phrygian, etc.)
  • Intervals: Naming, simple vs compound, inversion, enharmonic equivalents
  • Chords and Harmony: Triads and seventh chords, roman numeral analysis, figured bass, lead sheet symbols
  • Voice Leading and Part Writing: SATB, inversions, doubling, resolving dissonance, avoiding parallels
  • Harmonic Progressions: Common progressions, cadences, non-chord tones (passing, neighbor, suspensions, etc.)
  • Form and Analysis: Binary, ternary, rondo, sonata, phrase structures, motives, sequences
  • Aural Skills: Interval/chord recognition, melodic/rhythmic dictation, sight singing
  • Composition and Realization: Figured bass realization and writing short compositions

AP Music Appreciation

  • Elements of Music: Melody, harmony, rhythm, texture, form, dynamics, timbre, notation, instruments, and voice types
  • Historical Periods: Middle Ages (chant), Renaissance (polyphony), Baroque (fugue, opera), Classical (form, clarity), Romantic (emotion, nationalism), Modern (atonality, jazz)
  • World Music: African drumming, Indian raga, Gamelan, Native American music
  • Music in Society: Religion, politics, entertainment, cultural function
  • Listening Skills: Form/style recognition, instrument identification

Music Composition

  • Theory Foundations: Scales, chords, harmonic function, rhythm, counterpoint
  • Melody Writing: Motivic development, repetition, phrase structures, contour
  • Harmony/Accompaniment: Progressions, cadences, substitutions, textures
  • Orchestration: Instrument ranges, transposition, ensemble writing
  • Form: Binary, ternary, rondo, variations, sonata, film/game music structures
  • Software: MuseScore, Sibelius, Finale, formatting scores and parts
  • Analysis: Understanding structure, motifs, harmonic analysis

Music History

  • Western Periods: Medieval (chant, organum), Renaissance (polyphony), Baroque (opera, concerto), Classical (clarity, form), Romantic (emotion, program music), Modern (minimalism, electronic)
  • Key Composers: Bach, Handel, Vivaldi, Mozart, Haydn, Beethoven, Chopin, Verdi, Brahms, Debussy, Stravinsky, Schoenberg, Copland
  • Innovations: Instrument evolution, notation systems, tuning systems
  • Context: Music and politics, religion, culture, patronage, Renaissance humanism
  • Popular & World Influence: Folk music, jazz, rock, pop, genre crossover
