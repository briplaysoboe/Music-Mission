AP Music Theory

Course Overview: This course explores music notation, harmony, voice leading, analysis, and aural skills. Below are full-length lessons.

1. Fundamentals of Music Notation

Lesson: The staff consists of five lines and four spaces. Notes are placed on the staff to indicate pitch. The treble clef (G clef), bass clef (F clef), alto and tenor clefs help us locate notes. Ledger lines extend the staff above or below. Enharmonic equivalents are notes that sound the same but are written differently (e.g., C# and Db).

Watch: Music Notation Basics (YouTube)

2. Rhythm and Meter

Lesson: Rhythmic values include whole, half, quarter, eighth, and sixteenth notes and rests. Time signatures indicate beats per measure and beat value. Simple meters divide beats into two, compound into three, and irregular meters vary. Syncopation, ties, dots, and tuplets modify rhythm.

Watch: Understanding Rhythm and Meter

3. Scales and Modes

Lesson: Scales are collections of pitches arranged in ascending or descending order. Major scales follow the W-W-H-W-W-W-H pattern. Minor scales include natural, harmonic, and melodic forms. Modes include Dorian, Phrygian, Lydian, etc. Learn the circle of fifths for key relationships.

4. Intervals

Lesson: Intervals are the distances between two notes. Simple intervals are within one octave; compound intervals extend beyond. Learn to identify perfect, major, minor, augmented, and diminished intervals and their inversions.
